RetAllowance function

Actuarial Excel Addin

The RetAllowance function, returns, for employees with years of service before 1996, the portion of a retiring allowance that they may be able to directly transfer to a registered pension plan (RPP) or a registered retirement savings plan (RRSP) as per paragraph 60.j.1 of the Canada Income Tax Act .

* : Required parameter

Parameters
Default
Description
Date of hire *
--
.....
Date of termination *
--
Date of termination of employment
Date of membership *
--
Date of membership in the pension plan of the employer

Example #1
An employee terminates his employment on Jan.1, 2021. He was hired on July 1, 1991 and joined the employer's pension plan on July 1, 1992. How much of his retiring allowance can he transfer to his RRSP?

Date_of_hire = date(1991, 7, 1)

Date_of_membership = date(1992, 7, 1)

Date_of_termination = date(2021, 1, 1)

RetAllowance(Date_of_hire, Date_of_termination, Date_of_membership) returns 10000


Example #2
An employee terminates his employment on Jan.1, 2021. He was hired on July 1, 1985 and joined the employer's pension plan on July 1, 1987. How much of his retiring allowance can he transfer to his RRSP?

Date_of_hire = date(1985, 7, 1)

Date_of_membership = date(1987, 7, 1)

Date_of_termination = date(2021, 1, 1)

RetAllowance(Date_of_hire, Date_of_termination, Date_of_membership) returns 25000
